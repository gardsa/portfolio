Raise Hell, Babes - Documentary

video

I decided to center my documentary around Chloe Graham and her involvement in the all-women’s motorcycle network The Litas. I approached this project with a 3-step process: interview filming, B-roll filming, and then editing. To get a good idea of what I needed for my B-roll, I wanted to interview Chloe first to see what kind of material I could get from her, so that I could match that to a variety of relevant footage.

I wanted to achieve a number of things that I set out in my original proposal - establishing the riding environment, contextual juxtaposition of the voice-over with corresponding action in the footage, more handheld footage with intentional camera motion, and angles that are not the ‘norm’, such as low shots or close ups. My project output has successfully included all of these and more, and I am very happy with it.

I used a number of editing techniques, including: rhythmic editing, A/B roll intercutting, narrative audio, jump cuts, fast cutting, L/J cuts, establishing shots, POV shots, rack focus, and more. I wanted to use a variety of these techniques to really emphasise the dynamic side of my content and theme.

Overall, I am confident that my final video portrays a unique person with an interesting hobby/skill, and does so in a way that is not only informative, well thought out, and with great design intent, but is inspiring.
